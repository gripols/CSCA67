\subsubsection{2.1 For $n \ge 1, n^3 - n$ is divisible by 3}%
\label{ssub:2.1 For $n \ge 1, n^3 - n$ is divisible by 3}

Base (n = 1): $1^3 - 1 = 0$, which is divisible by 3.
Assume $k^3 - k = 3m$ for some integer $m$.
\begin{align*}
    (k+1)^3 - (k+1) &= (k^3 = 3k^3 + 3k + 1) - (k+1) \\
    &= (k^3 - k) + 3k^{2} + 3k \\
    &= 3m + 3(k^{2} + k) \\
    &= 3(m + k^2 + k) \\
\end{align*}

Which is divisible by 3, and therefore true by induction.
